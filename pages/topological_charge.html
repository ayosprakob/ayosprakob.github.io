About topological charge

Summary
Topological charge is an observable that measures non-perturbative property of the gauge field configuration. In even dimensions $d=2n$, the topological charge is given by $$ Q = \alpha_{2n}\int_\mathcal{M}d^{2n}x~\epsilon^{\mu_1\cdots\mu_d}~\text{Tr}~F_{\mu_1\mu_2}\cdots F_{\mu_{d-1}\mu_d} $$ where $\alpha_{2n}$ is some coefficient and $\mathcal{M}$ is the base manifold. It can be written more conveniently as a differential form as $$ Q=\alpha_{2n}\int_{\mathcal{M}}\text{Tr}~F^n. $$ The topological charge is quantized. In 2 dimensions, only $U(1)$ gauge theory has a non-trivial topological structure, while in 4 dimensions, only $SU(N)$ is allowed.
