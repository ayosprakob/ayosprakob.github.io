About topological charge

Summary
In $d=2n$ dimensions, the topological charge is given by $$ Q = \alpha_{2n}\int_\mathcal{M} \text{Tr}F^n. $$ Here, $F=\text{d}A+iA\wedge A$ is the curvature form, $F^{n}$ means the $n$-th power with respect to the exterior product, $\int_\mathcal{M}$ is the volume integral over the manifold $\mathcal{M}$, and $\alpha_{2n}$ is some coefficient that we will fix later. The integrand can be expressed as a sum of $2n$-forms $$ \text{Tr}F^n=\sum_{k=1}^{n} f_k\text{Tr}\{A^{2k-2}\wedge (\text{d}A)^{n-k+1}\} $$ Proof: We first write $$ F^n = \sum_{k=0}^n i^{k}~\text{Sf}( \underset{k}{\underbrace{A^2,\cdots,A^2}}, \underset{n-k}{\underbrace{\text{d}A,\cdots,\text{d}A}}) $$ where $\text{Sf}(~\cdot~)$ is the sum of all possible symmetric permutation of its arguments.
