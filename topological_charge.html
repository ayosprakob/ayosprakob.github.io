About topological charge

In this section, we will derive some known properties of the topological charge in even dimensions. At the beginning, I will use index-less notation for simplicity, then I will switch to the usual indexed notation when we discuss specific cases.

In $d=2n$ dimensions, the topological charge is given by $$ Q = \alpha_n\int \text{Tr}F^n. $$ Here, $F=\text{d}A+iA\wedge A$ is the curvature form, $F^{ n}$ means the $n$-th power with respect to the exterior product, $\int$ is the volume integral, and $\alpha_n$ is some coefficient that we will fix later. The integrand can be expressed as a sum of $2n$-forms $$ \text{Tr}F^n=\sum_{k=0}^{n} f_k\text{Tr}\{A^{2k}\wedge (\text{d}A)^{n-k}\} $$ for some coefficients $f_k$. Note that $$ A^{2k}\wedge (\text{d}A)^{n-k}=\frac{1}{2k+1}\text{d}(A^{2k+1}\wedge (\text{d}A)^{n-k-1}). $$ It follows that the topological charge is a total derivative \begin{align} Q &= \alpha_n\int \text{d}\omega_{2n-1};\\ \omega_{2n-1}&=\sum_{k=1}^n\frac{f_k}{2k-1}\text{Tr}\{A^{2k-1}\wedge (\text{d}A)^{n-k}\}. \end{align} The object $\omega_p$ is known as the Chern-Simon $p$-form.
