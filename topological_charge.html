Functional derivatives
Consider functional derivative of various quantities with respect to the gauge field $A^\mu_{ij}$ where $i$ and $j$ are the matrix indices and $\mu$ is the space-time component. Also let $B$ be some operator in the same Lie algebra as $A$. $$ \begin{align} \mathfrak{D}_{ij}^\mu(x)&:=\delta/\delta A_{ij}^\mu(x)\\ \mathfrak{D}_{ij}^\mu(x) A^\nu_{kl}(y)&:=\delta^{\mu\nu}\delta_{il}\delta_{jk}\delta(x-y)\\ \mathfrak{D}^\mu_{ij}(x)\text{Tr}\{A^\nu(y) B(y)\}&=\delta^{\mu\nu}B_{ij}(y)\delta(x-y). \end{align} $$ From now we will suppress the matrix indices and the position indices; the matrix indices of the results are the same as those of $\mathfrak{D}^\mu$ and we will always have the position of the differentiated matrix at $y$ and the derivative is with respect to $x$. The Dirac delta $\delta(x-y)$ will be shortened to $\delta_{xy}$ for simplicity. $$ \begin{align} \mathfrak{D}^\mu\text{Tr}\{A^\nu B\}&=\delta^{\mu\nu}B\delta_{xy},\\ \mathfrak{D}^\mu\text{Tr}\{\partial^\sigma A^\rho B\}&= \delta^{\mu\rho} B\partial^\sigma \delta_{xy},\\ \mathfrak{D}^\mu\text{Tr}\{[A^\sigma,A^\rho] B\}&= \delta^{\mu[\sigma,}[A^{\rho]},B] \delta_{xy},\\ \mathfrak{D}^\mu\text{Tr}\{F^{\sigma\rho} B\}&= i\delta^{\mu[\sigma,}[A^{\rho]},B] \delta_{xy} -B\delta^{\mu[\sigma,}\partial^{\rho]} \delta_{xy} \end{align} $$ We will now consider an operator of the form $$ Q=\alpha\int d^dx \text{Tr}\{F^{\mu\nu}G_{\mu\nu}\} $$ where $$ G_{\mu_1\mu_2} = \epsilon_{\mu_1\mu_2\mu_3\mu_4\cdots\mu_{d-1}\mu_{d}}F^{\mu_2\mu_3}\cdots F^{\mu_{d-1}\mu_d}. $$ Obviously, we are considering $d$ being even. Then we can perform the derivative on $Q$: $$ \begin{align} \mathfrak{D}^\rho Q&=\frac{1}{2}\alpha d\int d^dx \text{Tr} \{\mathfrak{D}^\rho F^{\mu\nu}G_{\mu\nu}\}\\ &=\frac{1}{2}\alpha d\int d^dx \{ i\delta^{\rho[\mu,}[A^{\nu]},G_{\mu\nu}] \delta_{xy} -G_{\mu\nu}\delta^{\rho[\mu,}\partial^{\nu]} \delta_{xy}\}\\ &=\alpha d\int d^dx \{ \partial^\mu G_{\rho\mu}+i[A^\mu,G_{\rho\mu}] \}\delta_{xy}\\ &=\alpha d D^\mu G_{\rho\mu} \end{align} where $D^\mu$ is the covariant derivative.
